# DUAL-BAND-STACKED-PATCH-ANTENNA-WITH-CROSS-SLOT-AND-CIRCULAR-POLARIZATION-FOR-GPS-APPLICATIONS-
This project presents the design and analysis of a Dual-Band Stacked Patch Antenna  with Cross-Slot and Circular Polarization using CST Microwave Studio. 
 In modern wireless 
communication and defence communication systems, there is an increasing demand for 
compact, lightweight, and high-efficiency antennas capable of operating over multiple 
frequency bands while maintaining stable radiation performance. Dual-band antennas are 
particularly important in satellite navigation and communication systems because they allow a 
single antenna structure to support multiple operating frequencies, thereby improving system 
flexibility and reducing overall hardware complexity. 
The proposed antenna design utilizes a stacked microstrip patch configuration along 
with a cross-slot structure and chamfered corners to achieve dual-band operation along with 
circular polarization characteristics. The stacked patch arrangement is to generate multiple 
resonant frequencies while maintaining compact antenna size. The cross-slot introduced in the 
radiating patch helps excite two orthogonal modes with the required phase difference necessary 
to generate circular polarization. corners of the patches are chamfered to further improve 
polarization characteristics and impedance matching. 
Circular polarization plays a crucial role in improving antenna radiation performance, 
especially in satellite and defence communication systems where the orientation between the 
transmitting and receiving antennas may continuously vary. The use of circular polarization 
reduces polarization mismatch losses, minimizes the effects of multipath interference, and 
ensures more reliable communication in dynamic operating environments. These features make 
the proposed antenna suitable for applications requiring stable and uninterrupted signal 
transmission. 
The antenna was designed and analyzed using CST Microwave Studio, which was used 
to model the complete antenna structure and study its electromagnetic behavior. During the 
design process, different antenna parameters such as patch dimensions, substrate size, slot 
dimensions, feed position, and the spacing between the stacked patches were carefully 
modified and optimized to achieve proper dual-band operation and good circular polarization 
performance. Special attention was given to the design of the cross-slot and chamfered corners, 
as these features play an important role in generating circular polarization and improving the 
overall radiation characteristics of the antenna. 
Several simulations were carried out in CST to observe how changes in the antenna 
geometry affected the performance parameters.  The stacked patch configuration was optimized to obtain multiple resonant frequencies while maintaining compact antenna 
dimensions. The simulation process also helped in improving impedance matching and 
reducing unwanted reflections within the desired frequency bands. In addition, the 
electromagnetic field distribution and current distribution on the antenna surface were studied 
to better understand the radiation mechanism and polarization behavior of the proposed design. 
The antenna performance was evaluated using important parameters such as return loss 
(S11), Voltage Standing Wave Ratio (VSWR), axial ratio, gain, directivity, bandwidth, and 
radiation pattern. These parameters were analyzed to verify the effectiveness of the proposed 
antenna design and to ensure stable communication performance. The simulation results 
confirmed that the antenna provides satisfactory dual-band characteristics, good circular 
polarization, and reliable radiation performance, making it suitable for GPS, satellite 
communication, and defence communication applications. 
The simulated results demonstrate that the proposed antenna successfully achieves 
dual-band operation with satisfactory circular polarization performance and good radiation 
characteristics. The antenna exhibits acceptable return loss, low VSWR, stable gain, and 
improved axial ratio within the desired operating bands. Due to these characteristics, the 
proposed antenna is highly suitable for applications such as GPS systems, satellite 
communication, wireless navigation systems, and defence communication networks where 
compact size, reliable signal transmission, and multi-band functionality are essential.
